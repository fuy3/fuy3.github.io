Guilin petroglyph

As an important part of Guilin's historical and cultural city, Guilin petroglyphs are one of the national key cultural relics protection units announced by the State Council and have high historical, artistic and scientific value.
However, with the passage of time, stone carvings have inevitably suffered damage from all aspects. These destructive factors mainly come from the natural environment and the impact of various human factors during the long-term preservation of the stone carvings. The overall protection of the existing Guilin stone carvings is not optimistic.
According to the survey results of the "Guilin Karst Geology Report" compiled by the Institute of Karst Geology, Chinese Academy of Geological Sciences, the average dissolution rate of carbonate rocks in Guilin is 87.88 mm/thousand years. A large part of the stone carving will disappear in natural denudation.
