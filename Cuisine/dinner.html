Dinner

Dinner are appropriate for the Chinese at both noon and in the evening. For weddings and other banquets in ordinary homes, noon may be a more traditional time to dine. For large parties, the evening is also a good choice. No matter what kind of banquet, a good meal is the most respectful expression of the guests.

China is a large country of food culture, for a long time in a certain area due to the geographical environment, climate and production, cultural traditions and ethnic customs and other factors, the formation of a certain degree of kinship inheritance relationship, the flavor of the dishes are similar, high visibility, and part of the masses like the local flavor of the famous school called cuisine. There are many schools of cuisine in cooking, among which the most influential and representative ones are recognized by the society: Lu, Guangdong, Sichuan, Suzhou, Zhejiang, Fujian, Hunan and Huizhou, known as the "eight major cuisines" in China.

Next, I will introduce a few famous traditional Chinese dishes:

Kung Pao chicken

“Kung Pao chicken” is a spicy, stir-fried Chinese dish made with cubes of chicken, peanuts, vegetables (traditionally Welsh onion only), and chili peppers. The classic dish in Sichuan cuisine originated in the Sichuan Province of south-western China and includes Sichuan peppercorns. Although the dish is found throughout China, there are regional variations that are typically less spicy than the Sichuan serving. Kung Pao chicken is also a staple of Westernized Chinese cuisine.

Cooking Steps:


Kung Pao chicken
  1. Chicken thighs boneless, peeled and washed, with the back of a knife to pat loose chicken meat, cut into 1.5 cm size dice, ginger and garlic slices, white onion cut into small circles.
  2. Add a little yellow wine and a little salt to the diced chicken, grab sticky with hands repeatedly, then put dry starch to grab well.
  3. Put sugar, rice vinegar, cooking wine, soy sauce and salt into the container and mix well, add onion, ginger and garlic, add the right amount of water and make the sauce.
  4. Heat the pot, pour in the oil, wait for the oil temperature of 70% hot, put the marinated chicken, stir-fry until the chicken turns white.
  5. A little oil left in the pot, add pepper, dried chili peppers, stir-fry over medium-low heat, the juice soaked onion, ginger and garlic, stir-fry in the pot for 10 seconds, then pour the chicken back into the pot, stir-fry over high heat for 30 seconds, pour in the juice, the color becomes oily, add chili oil, and finally add peanuts.

Mapo Tofu

Mapo tofu is also a popular Chinese dish from Sichuan province. It consists of tofu set in a spicy sauce, typically a thin, oily, and bright red suspension, based on douban (fermented broad bean and chili paste) and douchi (fermented black beans), along with minced meat, traditionally beef. Variations exist with other ingredients such as water chestnuts, onions, other vegetables, or wood ear fungus. One account indicates that the dish existed as early as 1254, in a suburb of Chengdu, the capital city of Sichuan province.

  1. Cut tofu into small pieces, put in a pot, blanch through with salt and soak in warm water.
  2. Wash green garlic, cut 1 cm long pieces, chop bean paste and black bean paste.
  3. Add oil to the pot until 60% hot, add the stuffed beef and stir dry the water, add the yellow wine, then add the bean paste and stir-fry the red oil.
  4. Add chili noodles, minced ginger, black bean paste and stir-fry, pour in stock, add tofu, add soy sauce, salt and cook the tofu through to taste with light heat.
  5. When the soup is slightly dry, add green garlic, water starch, reduce the juice, serve, sprinkle pepper powder can be.
