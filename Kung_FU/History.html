History

The genesis of Chinese martial arts has been attributed to the need for self-defence, hunting techniques and military training in ancient China. Hand-to-hand combat and weapons practice were important in training ancient Chinese soldiers, so martial arts are very important to them.

After experiencing primitive society, due to the improvement of people's manufacturing and metallurgical level, people made some close weapons to help them practice martial arts. The subsequent ancient Chinese feudal dynasties won by force and sought beauty by force. In court, officials were divided into civilian officials and military officials, and the Emperor treated them uniformly. "be famous for literary grace and warlike exploits" is the highest evaluation of a person, which may be the reason why Chinese martial arts have been popular in China for a long time.

However, the Song Dynasty implemented the national policy of "respecting literature and restricting martial arts", which made military attachés gradually inferior to civilian officials, and the power of the country became weaker and weaker. Chinese martial arts gradually declined.

Until Qing Dynasty—— the last dynasty in China, due to foreign aggression and the corruption and incompetence of the Qing government, some scholars abandoned their writings and tried to resist foreign aggression by force. In the Republic of China period, because China had just entered the age of firearms, Chinese martial arts received great restrictions, and the purpose of Chinese martial arts gradually became physical fitness.
